Learning to Window: Context-Aware HU Adaptation for Continual Trauma Injury Detection in CT Scans

CT scanners render tissue density using Hounsfield Units, and clinicians rely on "windowing" — mapping HU ranges to visible contrast — to see specific injuries clearly. Fixed windowing settings and static models struggle when imaging protocols, scanners, or patient populations shift over time. This work investigates a learnable windowing technique paired with context-aware HU adaptation, so trauma-injury detection models can continually adjust to this variability without full retraining or loss of previously learned diagnostic ability.

Motivation
Real-world CT protocols and patient populations shift over time; static models degrade.
Approach
Learnable windowing combined with context-aware Hounsfield Unit adaptation.
My role
